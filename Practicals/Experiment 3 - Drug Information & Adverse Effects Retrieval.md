# Experiment 3
## Retrieve the Information of a Drug and Its Adverse Effects Using Online Tools

**Software/Tools Used:** Web Browser + Online Drug Databases (MIMS India, Drugs.com, CDSCO, PubMed)

---

## 🎯 Objective

To search, retrieve, and compile structured drug information — including indications, dosage, and adverse drug reactions (ADRs) — using reliable online pharmaceutical databases, and present the findings in a clean reference document.

---

## 🧠 Why This Experiment Matters

A pharmacist must know not just where to find drug information, but **which sources are trustworthy**. This experiment trains you to navigate legitimate, evidence-based databases instead of relying on random Google results or unreliable health blogs — a skill you will use every single day in pharmacy practice.

---

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Choose a Drug to Research
For this exercise, we will use **Metformin** as the example drug. (In your own submission, you may choose any drug assigned by your faculty.)

### Step 2: Identify Reliable Online Sources

| Source | URL | Best For |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **MIMS India** | mims.com | Indian brand names, dosing, indications |
| **Drugs.com** | drugs.com | Detailed side effects, interactions |
| **CDSCO** | cdsco.gov.in | Indian regulatory status, approvals |
| **PubMed** | pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov | Clinical research, evidence |
| **WHO Essential Medicines List** | who.int | Global standard reference |

> 💬 Always prefer `.gov`, `.int`, recognised pharmaceutical databases, or peer-reviewed journals over random blogs or forums.

### Step 3: Search the Drug Name
1. Open a browser → go to **MIMS India** (mims.com) or **Drugs.com**
2. In the search bar, type: `Metformin`
3. Open the official drug monograph page

### Step 4: Record the Following Information Systematically

Create a table (in LibreOffice Writer or Calc) with these fields and fill them in as you find the information:

| Field | Information to Record |
|-------|------------------------|
| Generic Name | Metformin Hydrochloride |
| Brand Names (India) | Glycomet, Glucophage, Gluformin |
| Drug Category | Biguanide / Antidiabetic |
| Mechanism of Action | Decreases hepatic glucose production, increases insulin sensitivity |
| Indications | Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus |
| Standard Adult Dose | 500mg twice daily, titrated up to 2000mg/day |
| Route of Administration | Oral |
| Common Side Effects | Nausea, diarrhoea, abdominal discomfort, metallic taste |
| Serious Adverse Effects | Lactic acidosis (rare), Vitamin B12 deficiency (long-term) |
| Contraindications | Severe renal impairment (eGFR < 30), metabolic acidosis |
| Pregnancy Category | Generally considered safe; consult physician |
| Drug Interactions | Contrast dye (risk of renal impairment), Alcohol (lactic acidosis risk) |

### Step 5: Cross-Verify Adverse Effects on a Second Source
1. Open **Drugs.com** in a new tab
2. Search "Metformin side effects"
3. Compare the ADR list with what you recorded from MIMS
4. Note any additional ADRs found — especially rare/serious ones

### Step 6: Check Regulatory Status (India)
1. Visit **cdsco.gov.in**
2. Search for "Metformin" in the approved drug list (if portal search is available)
3. Note: Is it on the National List of Essential Medicines (NLEM)? Is it Schedule H (prescription-only)?

### Step 7: Compile a Final Drug Information Report
Open LibreOffice Writer and create a 1-page summary report using this structure:

```
DRUG INFORMATION REPORT

Drug Name: Metformin Hydrochloride
Category: Biguanide / Antidiabetic
Date Compiled: __________
Sources Used: MIMS India, Drugs.com, CDSCO

1. INDICATIONS
   [Write 2-3 lines]

2. DOSAGE
   [Write dosing details]

3. ADVERSE DRUG REACTIONS
   Common: [list]
   Serious: [list]

4. CONTRAINDICATIONS
   [list]

5. DRUG INTERACTIONS
   [list]

6. REGULATORY STATUS (INDIA)
   [Schedule classification, NLEM status]

References:
1. MIMS India — [URL]
2. Drugs.com — [URL]
3. CDSCO — [URL]
```

### Step 8: Save the Report
- File → Save As → `Metformin_Drug_Report.odt`
- Optionally export as PDF for submission

---

## ✅ Expected Output

A one-page, well-organized drug information report with properly cited sources, covering indications, dosage, ADRs (common and serious), contraindications, interactions, and Indian regulatory status.

---

## 📝 Viva/Record Questions

1. Why is it important to cross-verify drug information across at least two sources?
2. What is the difference between a "common" and a "serious" adverse drug reaction?
3. What does NLEM stand for, and why does it matter for an Indian pharmacist?
4. Name two signs that an online health source might NOT be reliable.

## 🔁 Practice Variation

Repeat this experiment for a different drug class — for example, an antibiotic (Amoxicillin) or an antihypertensive (Amlodipine) — and compare how the ADR severity and contraindication profile differs from Metformin.
